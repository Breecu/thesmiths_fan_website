ABOUT

The Smiths were an English rock band formed in 1982 in Manchester. The band consisted of Morrissey (vocals), Johnny Marr (guitar), Andy Rourke (bass), and Mike Joyce (drums). They were active from 1982 to 1987 and are known for their unique blend of indie rock, alternative rock, and post-punk. They released four studio albums and several hit songs, including "This Charming Man" and "How Soon Is Now?" which are considered among the greatest of the 1980s. They are often regarded as one of the most important bands of the decade and one of the most influential bands of all time.

The Smiths were formed in 1982 in Manchester, England, and quickly established themselves as one of the most important and influential bands of the 1980s. The band consisted of Morrissey on vocals, Johnny Marr on guitar, Andy Rourke on bass, and Mike Joyce on drums. Their unique blend of indie rock, alternative rock, and post-punk was characterized by Marr's distinctive guitar playing, Morrissey's poetic and often melancholic lyrics, and the band's overall aesthetic, which was heavily influenced by Northern England working-class culture. The Smiths released their debut album, "The Smiths," in 1984, which featured the hit single "This Charming Man." The album was well-received by critics and helped establish the band as one of the most exciting new acts in the UK. The band's follow-up album, "Meat Is Murder" was released in 1985, and included the hit single "That Joke Isn't Funny Anymore." The album reached number one on the UK Albums Chart and cemented the band's status as one of the most important and popular bands of the decade. Their third album, "The Queen Is Dead," was released in 1986, and is widely considered to be one of the greatest albums of all time. The album featured the hit single "There Is a Light That Never Goes Out" and was a commercial and critical success. The Smiths' final album, "Strangeways, Here We Come," was released in 1987. The band announced their break-up soon after, and despite several attempts to reform over the years, they have not reunited. The Smiths' influence has been felt across several generations of artists, and they are widely regarded as one of the most important and influential bands of all time. They are credited with helping to establish the indie rock and alternative rock genres and have been an inspiration to countless artists.
